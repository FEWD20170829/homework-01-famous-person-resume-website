Geena Davis

Actor | Film Producer | Writer | Academy Award Winner

Davis was born January 21, 1956, in Wareham, Massachusetts. At an early age, she became interested in music. She learned piano and flute and played organ well enough as a teenager to serve as an organist at her Congregationalist church.

Davis was working as a model when she was cast by director Sydney Pollack in his film Tootsie as a soap opera actress. She followed the role with the part of Wendy Killian in the short-lived television series Buffalo Bill which aired from June 1983 to March 1984.

After roles in Fletch, The Fly, Beetlejuice, and several other movies Davis received an Oscar as Best Supporting Actress for her appearance in The Accidental Tourist (1988), and a Best Actress nomination for her role in Thelma & Louise (1991). She replaced Debra Winger in the role of Dottie Hinson in A League of Their Own (1992), and received a Best Actress Golden Globe Award nomination for her performance.

Davis is also an activist against gender discrimination in US education institutions and a former Olympic Archer. She is married with four children.
